METHODOLOGY

Phase 1: Business Understanding

The primary objective was to understand customer purchasing behavior and identify opportunities for revenue growth through customer segmentation and purchasing trend analysis.

Business Questions:

* Which categories generate the highest revenue?
* Which customer groups contribute most to sales?
* How does subscription status influence spending?
* Which seasons generate the highest revenue?
* What customer segments should be prioritized?

Phase 2: Data Understanding

The dataset was explored to understand:

* Structure and dimensions
* Variable types
* Missing values
* Duplicate records
* Statistical characteristics

Phase 3: Data Preparation

Data cleaning activities included:

* Duplicate removal
* Missing value assessment
* Data type validation
* Standardization of categorical variables
* Data quality reporting

Phase 4: Feature Engineering

New analytical features were created to improve business understanding:

* Age Groups
* Purchase Levels
* Customer Value Score
* Subscriber Flag
* High-Value Customer Indicator
* Revenue Contribution Percentage
* Estimated Profit

These features enabled deeper segmentation and customer profiling.

Phase 5: Exploratory Data Analysis

Statistical analysis and visualizations were conducted to identify patterns and relationships within the data.

Techniques Included:

* Revenue Analysis
* Customer Demographics
* Seasonal Trend Analysis
* Category Performance Analysis
* Correlation Analysis

Phase 6: Business Intelligence and Reporting

Findings were translated into business recommendations focused on:

* Revenue optimization
* Customer retention
* Customer segmentation
* Marketing effectiveness
* Seasonal planning

The final deliverables included visualizations, reports, SQL analysis and actionable recommendations.
