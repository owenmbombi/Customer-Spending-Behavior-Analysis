EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Project Overview

The Retail Customer Purchase Analytics project was conducted to analyze customer purchasing behavior, identify revenue-generating customer segments and provide data-driven business recommendations. The dataset consisted of 2,005 customer records containing demographic information, purchasing activity, subscription status, product categories, shipping preferences and seasonal purchasing patterns.

The project followed a complete analytics workflow including data cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualization and business intelligence reporting using Python, SQL, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib and Seaborn.

Key Findings

The analysis revealed total revenue of $105 434.00 with an average purchase amount of $52.59 per customer. Clothing emerged as the dominant product category, generating approximately 62% of total revenue. Female customers accounted for nearly 60% of total sales while customers aged 18–25 represented the largest customer segment.

Subscription programs demonstrated a positive impact on customer spending behavior, with subscribers spending approximately 16% more than non-subscribers. Seasonal analysis indicated that Winter generated the highest revenue, contributing approximately 36% of overall sales.

Additionally, customers employed in creative and professional occupations such as Artists, Healthcare Workers, Engineers and Doctors generated the highest levels of revenue.

Business Recommendations

Based on the findings, the company should focus on expanding its Clothing category, increasing subscription adoption, strengthening winter marketing campaigns and implementing targeted promotions aimed at young adult and female customers. Furthermore, customer segmentation and loyalty programs should be introduced to improve customer retention and increase customer lifetime value.

Conclusion

The project demonstrates the practical application of data analytics techniques to solve business problems and generate actionable insights. Through statistical analysis, visualization and feature engineering, the project successfully identified revenue drivers, customer behavior patterns and growth opportunities that can support strategic decision-making.
